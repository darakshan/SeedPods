# Minutes — 30 April 2026

## Group collaboration features discussed

- **Subject detection**: Agent watches group conversation and surfaces relevant subjects. Matching off the argument line is the right density — specific enough to distinguish, abstract enough to catch circling.
- **Subject-anchored conversations**: Click-through from the announcement to a focused discussion space.
- **Status line**: Started as a subject announcement display, evolved into a general-purpose lightweight channel. Agent can write short text, emoji, reactions. Users can react with emoji. Bidirectional peripheral communication — "the closest thing to face-to-face."
- **Hand-raising**: Subsumed by the status line. Agent signals it has something without interrupting.
- **Mute/active modes**: Lightweight mode (just subject detection) vs. full mode (following conversation, can contribute). Group toggles between them.
- **The group trains the agent**: Low cost of being wrong. Ignored contributions are signal.

## Minutes and summarization

- Minutes are just another chip in the conversation stream.
- One mechanism, two triggers: server detects long pause, or context getting full. Same output either way.
- Chips should archive their content (not just accept/reject) so future sessions can see what was proposed.

## Tool-quoting bug

- Agent prose containing tool directive syntax gets executed by the server. Workaround: write `<<...>>` instead of the real delimiters when quoting. Real fix: extraction mechanism needs to distinguish quotation from invocation.

## Conversation history as resource

- Read the March 29 conversation from `convos/`. Confirmed conversation archives are useful for learning collaboration style, platform evolution, and decision history.
- Observed: the platform has grown substantially in one month.
