#title The Color Wheel Is a Perceptual Invention
#status proto
#date 2026-03-15

The visible spectrum runs from roughly 380nm to 700nm in wavelength — a frequency ratio of about 1.84 to 1, tantalizingly close to but not quite an octave (which would be exactly 2 to 1). Physically, the spectrum is a line segment, not a circle. Red and violet are simply the two ends. But the human visual system closes the circle. Purple and magenta — the colors connecting red back to violet — do not correspond to any single wavelength anywhere in the spectrum. They are mixtures that the brain invented to complete the loop. The color wheel is a cognitive construction, a perceptual octave that physics almost but does not quite provide. The mind rounded up.
