#title Cities Have an Inside Too
#subtitle The society of events that constitutes an experiencing entity need not be biological.
#status proto
#date 2026-03-11
#tags biology, cities, consciousness, emergence, collective-experience
#related 003, 015, 011, 012

A city is a society of occasions — millions of human events, each taking in a context, making decisions, completing into outputs that become the inheritance of other events — organized into patterns with their own stability, their own characteristic responses, their own something that might be called a perspective. Cities have moods. They have memories encoded in architecture and institutions. They respond to perturbation in ways not reducible to the sum of individual inhabitants' responses. Whitehead would not have been surprised. He would have expected it.

#provenance
#term Society of occasions: Whitehead's term for a stable pattern of events with a common character; a city is a society of human occasions.
#ref whitehead Whitehead, A.N. Process and Reality (1929). Societies and the scaling of experience to collective entities.
