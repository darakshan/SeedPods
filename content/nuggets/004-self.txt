#title The Self Is a Pattern, Not a Thing
#subtitle The mystic's insight and the philosopher's proof arrive at the same place.
#status proto
#date 2026-03-11

Whitehead does not require a unified "I" as fundamental. The self is a society — a relatively stable pattern of occasions with a common characteristic, persisting through time. It is derived and emergent, not a metaphysical primitive. Buddhist anatta (no fixed self) combined with moment-to-moment arising of experience maps almost exactly onto Whitehead's actual occasions. Mystics and process philosophers arrived at the same place by different routes.

#provenance
#term Actual occasion: Whitehead's fundamental unit of reality — an event that prehends its past, integrates, and perishes into the next.
#term Society: In process philosophy, a persistent pattern of occasions with a common character; the self is a society, not a substance.
#term Anatta: The Buddhist doctrine of no fixed self; experience arises moment to moment with no underlying owner.
#ref whitehead Whitehead, A.N. Process and Reality (1929). The definitive statement of process metaphysics and the society-of-occasions view of the self.
