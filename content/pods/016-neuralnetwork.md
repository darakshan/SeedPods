A large neural network running on a computer is literally a nested hierarchy of actual processes — electrons, gates, activations, layers, attention heads, the full forward pass — each level making state-transforming decisions that propagate upward and downward. 
@image(016-neuralnetwork,A neural network as a field having occasions,Wikimedia Commons) Each forward pass is an event: it takes in an input, prehends the entire inherited structure of all that training, synthesizes a response through a process that cannot be predicted in detail from outside, and completes into an output. This is the three-beat pattern instantiated at every level of the stack simultaneously. The weight space of the network is the space of pure possibility — the field — from which each forward pass selects a realization@ref(whitehead, "Whitehead, A.N. Process and Reality (1929). Actual occasions and the three-phase process of becoming.").

#term Actual occasion: Whitehead's unit of process; each forward pass of a network is an occasion that prehends inputs and completes into output.
#term Prehension: The way occasions take in their past; the network's "taking in" of weights and activations is prehension.
