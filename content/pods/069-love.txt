Love is also a phenomenon. A phenomenological condition with a physics-like structure.

Love is a phenomenon of light — specifically, of the kind that draws you toward it until it destroys you. Icarus. Looking at the sun. Flying. The flame and the moth, burning up in love.

And yet: in the zone, falling in love, lost in space. There is a phenomenology of the approach — the narrowing of attention, the suspension of self, the sense of being drawn into an orbit.

The same image serves: looking at the sun. Too much and you are blinded. At the right angle, everything is illuminated.

The illustration is already there. It connects temperature, light, and love into a single phenomenological cluster.
