# About Seed Nuggets

The deepest purpose of this project is intuition repair — our own as much as anyone's. Most of these seed nuggets are not introducing new information. They are revisiting assumptions so familiar they've become invisible. Each seed nugget earns its place by containing a genuine surprise: a moment where the world turns out to be slightly different from what we'd been assuming. The surprise is the point.

### Acknowledgements

This collection is partly inspired by Doug Hofstadter's amazing 1979 book Gödel, Escher, Bach.  It's a romp though many of the same ideas, but we've come along way.  It's also inspired by the rich and unde-appreciated philosophy of Alfred North Whitehead, such as Process and Reality published in 1929.  Both of these works only benefit from our modern perspective and all the advancements since they were written.

 The format of Seed Nuggets is inspired by Christopher Alexander's classic "A Pattern Language", which was written well before the invention of hyperlinks and the advent of global internet access, but clearly foresees that in its organization. The reader can choose their own path through the adventure.
 
 Some day, inshallah, some or all of these nuggets will turned into a book, or a movie, or a series of articles, or who knows what, in a linear progression. Time will tell.
