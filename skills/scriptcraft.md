# Scriptcraft

For folios with a section that holds a short film outline (4–8 scenes). This is an outline — the shooting script lives elsewhere.

## Format

A numbered list of scenes. Each entry: the visual direction and any narration together on one line or two. If there are more than eight, you're writing a screenplay, not an outline.

## Voiceover

Sparingly — at most two VO moments in a three-minute film. If VO appears in every scene you're writing a narrated slideshow. If the VO can be cut without losing the scene, cut it.

## Show, don't tell

- POV is the most powerful tool for an interior. Put the audience inside the experience; they'll feel it before they understand it.
- Sound design carries turns no narration can — a shift from muffled to clear can be the climax.
- Let objects do narrative work: a phone becoming a smartphone, hair going white. Worth ten years of title cards.

## Compose for one screen

A three-minute outline should fit on one screen. If it doesn't, it hasn't been edited. Camera angles, exact dialogue, performance notes, scene-by-scene music cues all belong in the shooting script, not here.

## End on an image

The final frame should stand alone as a still. Text-on-black is a last resort.
