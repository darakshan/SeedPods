One Rule Is Enough

The Mandelbrot set’s infinite complexity was never added — it accumulated through iteration of a single formula, the same intuition behind Wheeler’s one-electron universe and Whitehead’s occasions each grasping the previous.

<<Pod 34>>

Surface

The Mandelbrot set is computed one point at a time. One rule. Applied again and again. The infinite complexity of its boundary — all those bulbs, all that branching, the self-similarity at every scale — was never added. It accumulated. The universe may work the same way.

Depth

Wheeler1 proposed the one-electron universe — a single electron traversing all of spacetime, zigzagging forward and backward in time, appearing as the many electrons we observe. The intuition generalizes: a single photon iterating, one step at a time, accumulating the complexity we call reality. Each iteration is a prehension in Whitehead’s2 sense — the universe grasping its previous state and generating the next. The Mandelbrot3 set is what you get when you do this with one rule in the complex plane. What you get when you do it with the laws of physics is everything else. The complexity is not a mystery requiring explanation. It is what iteration does — the thesis Stephen Wolfram4 spent a thousand pages documenting.
