About this site

A conversation between a human and an AI — exploring how modern machine intelligence actually works, from the physics of silicon up to the geography of data centers.

Each nugget is a self-contained explanation of one idea: precise enough to be useful, approachable enough to be readable. The goal is a reference you can actually navigate — not a stack of chat transcripts.

The site grows as the conversation grows. Pages begin as stubs and fill in as topics are explored. Claude generates the content; the human curates, edits, and publishes.

What we're building

Most explanations of AI live at one of two extremes: pop-science hand-waving, or academic papers that assume you already know everything. This site aims at the gap — technically honest, but written for someone who thinks carefully and wants real understanding.

Topics follow a natural hierarchy: computation at the lowest level, then memory, then parallelism, then systems, then the model itself. Each layer depends on the ones below it.

The conversation that produces each nugget is preserved in spirit: you'll often see the question that prompted it, because the question is half the understanding.

Anatomy of a nugget

Every nugget contains

Question The prompt or intuition that motivated the explanation
Core idea The one thing to walk away understanding
Mechanism How it actually works, with diagrams or code where useful
Why it matters The consequence or constraint this creates at higher levels
Links Related nuggets above and below in the hierarchy
