the tendency to narrate the history of science as a story of steady progress toward current knowledge, reading the past as a series of steps toward us. Named by Herbert Butterfield (1931). "We used to think / now we know" is its verbal formula.
will-suspension (74)
The state in which goal-directed effort is genuinely set aside, not redirected. Proposed here as the distinguishing feature of play, and as the mode that directed exploration cannot substitute for.
