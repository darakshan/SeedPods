@argument
A neural network is not analogous to Whitehead's picture but a concrete instance of it, each forward pass prehends the entire inherited structure of training, synthesizes a response through a process not predictable from outside, and completes into output.

@section(proto)
A large neural network running on a computer is literally a nested hierarchy of actual processes, electrons, gates, activations, layers, attention heads, the full forward pass, each level making state-transforming decisions that propagate upward and downward.
@image(016-neuralnetwork,A neural network as a field having occasions,Wikimedia Commons) Each forward pass is an event: it takes in an input, prehends the entire inherited structure of all that training, synthesizes a response through a process that cannot be predicted in detail from outside, and completes into an output. This is the three-beat pattern instantiated at every level of the stack simultaneously. The weight space of the network is the space of pure possibility, the field, from which each forward pass selects a realization@footnote(@ref(whitehead, "Whitehead, A.N. Process and Reality. Macmillan, 1929.") The three-phase process of becoming, each actual occasion takes in its world, integrates it through a process of internal determination, and completes into a definite outcome. The forward pass instantiates this at every level of the network stack simultaneously.).

@term Actual occasion: Whitehead's unit of process; each forward pass of a network is an occasion that prehends inputs and completes into output.
@term Prehension: The way occasions take in their past; the network's "taking in" of weights and activations is prehension.
