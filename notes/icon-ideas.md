Global System Rules (apply to all 7)

    Style: Monochrome, outline only

    Stroke: Uniform (e.g., 2px at 24×24, scales proportionally)

    Caps/joins: Rounded

    Grid: Designed on a square 32×32

    Padding: Equal margin on all sides (~10%)

    No fills, no shading, no gradients

    Max complexity: ~5–8 primitives per icon

    Visual weight: Each icon should feel equally “dense” at small size

1. AI Minds — “5-node network”

Geometry:

    Exactly 5 circular nodes

        1 node connected to

        4 surrounding nodes in an irregular (not perfectly symmetric) arrangement

    Straight line connections:

        Center connects to all 4 outer nodes

        At least 2 outer-to-outer connections (not fully connected graph)

Constraints:

    Node size small but clearly visible

    Lines do not overlap nodes awkwardly (clean joins)

    Slight asymmetry to avoid “corporate diagram stiffness”

Intent:
A thinking structure, not a circuit board, not a brain.
2. Biology — “single DNA strand (simplified)”

Geometry:

    One vertical double-curve (helix suggestion)

        Two parallel sinusoidal lines (very subtle, not exaggerated)

    3–4 short horizontal “rungs” connecting the দুই sides

Constraints:

    Keep it abstract, not textbook DNA

    No twist shading, no perspective

    Fits comfortably in vertical center

Intent:
Life pattern / organic code, not biotech branding.
3. Consciousness — “minimal spiral”

Geometry:

    Single continuous spiral line

    ~1.5 to 2 turns max

    Starts near center, expands outward

Constraints:

    Even spacing between turns

    Line weight consistent (no tapering)

    Ends cleanly (not decorative flourish)

Intent:
Awareness unfolding—simple, inward → outward structure.
4. Knowledge — “open book (reduced)”

Geometry:

    Symmetric V-shaped base (spine)

    Two angled planes (left/right pages)

    Optional: 1 very subtle inner line per page (but can omit)

Constraints:

    No text lines clutter

    Avoid “cartoon book” look (keep it geometric)

    Spine is implied, not thick

Intent:
Stored, structured knowledge—not storytelling, not decoration.
5. Mathematics — “axes + diamond”

Geometry:

    Horizontal axis (left–right line with arrowheads)

    Vertical axis (bottom–top with arrowheads)

    Small “x” label at right end

    Small “y” label at top end

    Centered diamond (rotated square) outline

Constraints:

    Diamond centered exactly at origin

    Diamond size modest (not touching axes)

    Labels minimal, clean sans-serif style if rendered as text, or geometric approximations

Intent:
Abstract coordinate reasoning—math as structure, not symbols like π.
6. Physics — “field lines”

Geometry:

    Central empty circle (object/source)

    4–6 curved lines flowing around it symmetrically

        Think magnetic field, not orbital paths

Constraints:

    Lines should flow, not intersect

    Maintain even spacing

    No arrows unless extremely subtle (optional)

Intent:
Forces and fields—continuous influence, not particles.
7. Sensation — “contact + propagation”

Geometry:

    One small solid or outlined contact point

    2–3 concentric partial arcs radiating outward

        Not full circles—open arcs

Constraints:

    Arcs centered on the point

    Increasing radius, consistent spacing

    Avoid symmetry that looks like a “button” or UI tap

Intent:
Signal entering awareness—contact → spreading perception.
Final sanity check (important)

If you lay them out side by side, they should feel like:

    Same hand drew them

    Same system of thought

    No icon feels more detailed than the others

    Each is distinguishable at 16px
