The Self Is a Pattern, Not a Thing

The mystic's insight and the philosopher's proof arrive at the same place.

whitehead buddhism mysticism identity self

Depth

Whitehead does not require a unified "I" as fundamental. The self is a society — a relatively stable pattern of occasions with a common characteristic, persisting through time. It is derived and emergent, not a metaphysical primitive. Buddhist anatta (no fixed self) combined with moment-to-moment arising of experience maps almost exactly onto Whitehead's actual occasions. Mystics and process philosophers arrived at the same place by different routes.
