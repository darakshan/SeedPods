The Field Is the Medium of Feeling

Physics and Whitehead converged on the same discovery from opposite directions.

physics whitehead fields quantum-mechanics prehension

Depth

A quantum field pervades all of space. It is not made of things; it is a plenum of potential from which actual events are drawn. What we call a particle is a localized event in that field — a moment of excitation. Whitehead proposed, on purely philosophical grounds, that events are primary and enduring things are the abstraction. Physics arrived at the same conclusion through experiment. The convergence is not accidental. They were both following the argument where it leads. The field just is the medium through which occasions prehend each other.
