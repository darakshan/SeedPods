Nothing Was Added — The Numbers Just Got Large Enough

Every threshold that looks like a miracle from outside is just accumulation crossing a point.

emergence complexity consciousness evolution

Depth

The materialist assumes that at some point something must have been added — a life force, a soul, a ghost in the machine. The Whiteheadian answer is: nothing was added. The occasions accumulated. Tiny asymmetries compounded. At sufficient scales of complexity, something emerges that can look back at the pattern and recognize itself in it. This is not magic. It is what accumulation does. The creative advance does not need a blueprint. It needs only the irreducible novelty of each event meeting its inherited world.
