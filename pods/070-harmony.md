Pythagoras (c. 570–495 BCE) heard the ratios in the hammers. But the circle of fifths doesn't close — stack twelve perfect fifths and you don't return to where you started. The irrational intrudes. The curse of the irrational.

Equal temperament is the solution: close the circle by distributing the error equally across all twelve steps. It works. It also means no interval except the octave is pure. Every fifth is slightly wrong. Every third is slightly wrong. We traded perfection for closure.

But there is another basis. Thirds and fifths together span a two-dimensional space. The triangular grid of just intonation — not a circle but a lattice, infinite in principle, navigated by ear. Jacob Collier lives here.

Consonance as harmonious relationship. The same ratios that make intervals pleasing make proportions in visual art feel right. The connection between harmonious sound and harmonious sight is not metaphor — it is the same mathematical structure appearing in two sensory channels.

Harmonious relationships in art are beauty.
