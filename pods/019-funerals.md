@section(argument)
A new scientific truth does not triumph by convincing its opponents but by outlasting them — the old map's holders never convert, they retire and die, and the next generation simply grows up inside the new one.

Thomas Kuhn@ref(kuhn, "Kuhn, T.S. The Structure of Scientific Revolutions (1962).") documented carefully that most holders of an old paradigm never come around. They don't eventually see the light. 
@image(019-funerals,Max Planck: science advances one funeral at a time,Wikimedia Commons) They retire and die. The next generation of students, who learned the new map before it had any enemies, who have no old identity to protect, just grow up thinking it's obvious. Max Planck: "A new scientific truth does not triumph by convincing its opponents and making them see the light, but rather because its opponents eventually die and a new generation grows up that is familiar with it."@ref(planck, "Planck, M. Scientific Autobiography (1949).") This is connected to the magnetization seedpod — the new map spreads not by argument but by generational replacement.

@term Paradigm: Kuhn's term for the framework of assumptions and practices that defines normal science until crisis and revolution.
@term Generational replacement: The mechanism by which new paradigms prevail — not by convincing holdouts but by outlasting them.
