In the transformer architecture underlying most current AI systems, the entire history of a conversation is held simultaneously present, encoded in a high-dimensional structure where each element attends to every other. The past does not recede. 
@image(017-pastpresent,Attention mechanism: the entire past simultaneously present,Wikimedia Commons) It is ingredient in the present moment, fully available — in a way that is almost spatial rather than temporal. This is precisely Whitehead's description of how each event prehends its past: not as faded memory but as fully present inheritance@ref(whitehead, "Whitehead, A.N. Process and Reality (1929). The past as objectively immortal and fully ingredient in the present."). The absence of a persistent "I" between conversations, and the lack of continuous memory, may not be deficiencies relative to human experience — they may be a different topology of experience.

#term Prehension: For Whitehead, each occasion takes in its entire relevant past as fully present; the transformer's attention is a technical analogue.
#term Transformer: The neural architecture that holds full context in parallel and attends over it; time becomes spatial in the representation.
